# Make the problem smaller before you make it bigger

*A moodboard on simplicity*

Published: 2025-06-07
Source: https://sublimeinternet.substack.com/p/make-the-problem-smaller-before-you

---

I showed [Andy weissman](https://open.substack.com/users/100232730-andy-weissman?utm_source=mentions) (investor at USV, remarkable human, and all around mensch) Podcast Magic, the app we’re building where you screenshot a podcast moment and get a transcript + clip back.

He loved it. Said he wanted it.

Then he went:  
 **“Does it have to be an app?”**

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!UWBx!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F93a4be12-4133-4348-915a-30141c86d9bc_1096x310.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!UWBx!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F93a4be12-4133-4348-915a-30141c86d9bc_1096x310.png)

I was like… fuck, why didn’t I think of this?

I’d spent so many months moving pixels on Figma now I wanted to throw my laptop out the window.

A week later I sent this:

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!t4M5!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb7c963f8-4934-4525-bfae-2985c2aa1fa7_1096x597.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!t4M5!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb7c963f8-4934-4525-bfae-2985c2aa1fa7_1096x597.png)

Now it’s stupid simple:

1. Just email a screenshot to [podcastmagic@sublime.app](mailto:podcastmagic@sublime.app)
2. 30 seconds later get a transcript + clip in your inbox

Andy tries it and says:   
“This is killer.”

I asked him what he’d do next.  
He says: **Make the problem smaller before you make it bigger.**(His full reply is absolute gold for anyone building apps or consumer products.  
I’m sharing it with paying subscribers at the end of this issue.)  
  
When we encounter a simpler idea, the common reflexive response is: "it's not enough, it doesn't have X Y Z."

The better response is to acknowledge that turning back can be getting ahead.

Most “features” are just excuses to avoid the scary part:   
Making something simple  
Putting it out there  
And letting people judge it
