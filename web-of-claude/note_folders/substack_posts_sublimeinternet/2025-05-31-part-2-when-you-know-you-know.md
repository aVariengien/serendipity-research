### The Risk

With little over two weeks before launching Sublime to the world, Sari and Gabriel had a decision to make.

They could have kept the design of the new website in-house, stuck to the plan, and got it done.

Or they could put out feelers…

Well, one feeler.

To David Anastácio, a designer who had been in Gabriel’s orbit since 2022 and who he admired. He’d discovered David’s work via [the short film](https://youtu.be/ySEqyDp7nBQ) he'd made for Rekki. Whoever cared to invest in this sort of thing, Gabriel thought, must know what's up.

## Week One

With a two-week deadline before launch, most designers would move fast and dive into Figma—design, iterate, and deliver.

A soft-spoken artist covered in a precise arrangement of tattoos, David did the opposite.

He spent the first few days asking questions, reading everything we'd written—articles, testimonials, stray notes, love notes—everything.

He cared for the story, the feeling, the frequency of it all.

What could Sari and Gabriel do but trust that the visuals would come.

I asked Sari if she felt nervous.

> *"I did worry about the deadline because the first week was very divergent, but I didn't feel fear, I let the process unfold because I felt this peace that comes from knowing you are in great hands."*

And so, in David they trusted.

Meanwhile, David was playing around with new words and language to describe Sublime in, of all places, Cursor, the AI-powered code editor. (??!!)

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0eQd!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffa8e8fd1-34a2-483e-b0ab-ceb6d25cdca6_3218x2094.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0eQd!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffa8e8fd1-34a2-483e-b0ab-ceb6d25cdca6_3218x2094.png)

David and Gabriel work on brand voice exploration.

He'd loaded the code editor thing with everything he'd found; brand voices he admired, what we’d shared, random raw resources…and all that served as the foundation to then start prompting the AI.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!VvQD!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7b25b4c0-5bf7-4720-b7b2-d05b6d742f52_1650x1956.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!VvQD!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7b25b4c0-5bf7-4720-b7b2-d05b6d742f52_1650x1956.png)

All files David fed to Cursor.

David knew what he wanted; something…Short. Poetic. Punchy.

Something like this:

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Z2To!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F72b37102-e5cd-41ca-a124-33e89e7ea12f_1604x320.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Z2To!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F72b37102-e5cd-41ca-a124-33e89e7ea12f_1604x320.png)

Having fed all of that context to Cursor, he started prompting the AI. He refined and refined some more. V2 became V27. Finally, things started feeling right.

He dropped the poetic brand statements into a shared Sublime collection.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7J7O!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7c0ee950-cb14-4fe6-a9e7-c9b27098e213_3216x2092.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7J7O!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7c0ee950-cb14-4fe6-a9e7-c9b27098e213_3216x2092.png)

*"I LOVED these,"* Sari said.

> *"I'm a words person and he was capturing the feeling of the brand with words in a way that impressed me, which rarely happens. It was just such a clear example of using AI to make work with soul."*

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!SeE1!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb24eb6e1-fba8-4f56-858f-59a7168c2291_5632x3820.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!SeE1!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb24eb6e1-fba8-4f56-858f-59a7168c2291_5632x3820.png)

Sample capture of the feeling of our brand in words.

Why spend time on words that wouldn’t make it onto the landing page itself?   
  
Because that wasn’t the point. The poetic exercise was a way in—not into copy, but into a shared understanding of what Sublime could be.

The ground was fertile. The good stuff could grow.

## No Ego

Sari and Gabriel thought they’d now dive into visuals and get the website done.

But no, not exactly. Not just yet.

David became obsessed with [the Sublime logo (designed by Bate](https://sublimeinternet.substack.com/p/the-story-of-sublimes-logo), the creative studio we'd worked with for our first landing page.)

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Vff1!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fec1da834-7051-4191-85b2-6d48fb84d70f_5168x1500.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Vff1!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fec1da834-7051-4191-85b2-6d48fb84d70f_5168x1500.png)

Sublime’s logo and wordmark designed by Bate. David felt these were the protagonists of our new website.

There was something about the logo that kept pulling him in. It didn’t feel SaaS. It was layered. In an act devoid of ego, David took this symbol, this thing made by other designers, and expanded on its essence.

He carved a 3D object out of it, and started playing with this crazy AI tool called ComfyUI to transform the logo into various materials.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!K0Eq!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9c8a2af5-7886-4153-9491-58a910c9abb3_3218x2094.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!K0Eq!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9c8a2af5-7886-4153-9491-58a910c9abb3_3218x2094.png)

*(Paying subs get a video clip where David explains how you can turn your 2D logo into a living, breathing 3D material object)*

Concrete, glass, chrome, gold, corn, ice. Every few hours, a new image landed in Slack.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!kNv6!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff2a0175b-1301-40d5-8e5d-ca0277ca1aa5_4812x2334.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!kNv6!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff2a0175b-1301-40d5-8e5d-ca0277ca1aa5_4812x2334.png)

Sublime wordmark with various treatments.

But one felt most alive: moss, green, rocks, earthy stuff.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Doe6!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F42746c22-07d0-4fb5-8d65-bf2152640dbd_5464x1704.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Doe6!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F42746c22-07d0-4fb5-8d65-bf2152640dbd_5464x1704.png)

The moss/natural application, we felt, most embodied the spirit of Sublime.

This right here, this was it.

We’d long believed that tending to your knowledge mirrored tending to a garden—the earthy, messy care and love of a place where the result is unknown—some ideas wither, others thrive.

Now, our logo embodied it.

Except wait. The H1 "not another boring knowledge tool" no longer made sense.

What about "The knowledge tool that sparks joy"?

Closer. Good, at least for now.

## When You Think It’s Done…

As things fell into place we looked at the headline again—'the knowledge tool that sparks joy' and something felt off.

Sari looked back at the Mario meme.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Vir7!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe46a67d4-de67-4403-a752-e70d906e9e8d_1456x1115.webp)](https://sublime.app/card/part-1-finding-your-thing-s-essence-89607765315d45e0-image)

What did Sublime help Mario become?

Creative.

*Joy* was broad, *creativity* felt specific.

[Obvious in hindsight](https://sublime.app/card/190909-jpeg), nailing the headline took time. The process mirrored the way we’d built the entire landing page: follow the feeling.

In the coming age of AI-assisted work, vision becomes everything. Without vision, you end up with iteration soup. With it, you can take risks knowing that while you may not know exactly where you’re headed, you’ll know it when you get there.

When you know, you know.  
And finally, we knew.
