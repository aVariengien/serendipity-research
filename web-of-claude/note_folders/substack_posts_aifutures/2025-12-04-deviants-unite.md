# Deviants Unite!

Published: 2025-12-04
Source: https://aifutures.substack.com/p/deviants-unite

---

Wow do I ever love this analysis in my new favourite Substack, [Experimental History](https://www.experimental-history.com/) by Adam Mastroianni, especially this post [The Decline of Deviance](https://www.experimental-history.com/p/the-decline-of-deviance), in which he outlines (through detailed research that includes a lot of fabulous infographics) that people aren’t weird enough anymore.

His point, as I read it, is that when life was more dangerous and unpredictable by default (citing things like lower life expectancy, higher infant mortality, more violent conflicts) there was greater space for accidental deviance because people had less to lose.

When institutions were slower and less centralized, it was easier to drift between communities, join fringe groups, or live on the margins, so people ended up wandering around the countryside behaving eccentrically or getting involved with the cults and subcultures that were flourishing in the baked tundra miles off of Route 66 (or both).

I admit that on some level this seems counterintuitive to me because the entire world right now seems like something that could be happening in a parking lot behind an appalling motel, which includes a paradigm\-busting attack on the institutions that have in the past anchored us, but Mastroianni’s data on long term trends is convincing and the ultimate point here is that we need more of the good kind of weird if we are going to escape from the deep sameness that we can feel on the daily, and I am all in on that.

He cites [The Age of Average](https://www.alexmurrell.co.uk/articles/the-age-of-average?utm_source=substack&utm_medium=email) by Alex Murrell, which I also wrote about in T[he Inexplicable Charm of the Flat and Same](https://aifutures.substack.com/p/the-inexplicable-charm-of-the-flat), and addresses the paucity of glamour and ornament in modern cities that I got worked up about in my post [Put a Gargoyle on it](https://aifutures.substack.com/p/put-a-gargoyle-on-it).

To bring it to AI, I am thinking about [AI Film](https://aifutures.substack.com/p/unaddressed-contours-on-the-lot) as one example of where there is a both a game\-changing power shift in how moving images are being made and a stultifying reenactment, historical civil war style, of content that we have all not only seen exactly twelve million times before but that is itself redolent of what Brian Klaas of [The Garden of Forking Paths](https://www.forkingpaths.co/) calls [The Age of the Surefire Mediocre](https://www.forkingpaths.co/p/the-age-of-the-surefire-mediocre).

##### This is probably happening in the United States somewhere right now

But anyway all of this is why I find Mastroianni’s thesis energizing rather than bleak. We must turn to actively engineering the weird if we are to get anywhere on this. It’s why I love what people are doing with AI in contemporary art practice, and why I love the early twelve\-fingered models.
