# Gemformatics

*A way forward for jewelery and fashion*

Published: 2024-12-06
Source: https://aifutures.substack.com/p/gemformatics

---

The use of data as a material, concept, or subject in media art emerged in the mid to late 20th century, influenced by the rise of digital technologies and computational systems, so it’s not a new thing.

Thinking of everyone from artists like [Vera Molnár](http://www.veramolnar.com/) and [Herbert W. Franke](https://www.katevassgalerie.com/math-art-by-herbert-w-franke) who began with generative art in the 1950, 60s, or even [John Cage](https://www.johncage.org/) and his interest in algorithms as a method to produce chance based operations. In the 1990s we have internet artists like [JODI](https://net-art.org/jodi), incorporating network sensibilities, web data, and real\-time inputs into their work or [Cornelia Sollfrank](https://www.katevassgalerie.com/blog/happy-b-day-cornelia-sollfrank) and her net art generators that scraped and remixed webpages, or [Monika Fleischmann \& Wolfgang Strauss](https://www.fleischmann-strauss.de/), and the emergence of Data Visualization as an aesthetic practice and I always think of [Jer Thorp](https://www.jerthorp.me/), [Ben Fry](https://benfry.com/), [Casey Reas](https://reas.com/), [Mark Hansen](https://journalism.columbia.edu/faculty/mark-hansen), [Ben Rubin](https://www.earstudio.com/) et al for that. Then artists like [Rafael Lozano\-Hemmer](https://www.lozano-hemmer.com/) and [Zach Lieberman](http://zach.li/) who used gesture or bio\-informatic data to create responsive installations \- many engaging with notions of the quantified self \- commentary, critique etc. about the increasing importance of data in human lives. Then let’s skip to people like [Refik Anadol](https://refikanadol.com/) whose work builds on advances in AI and machine learning in order to use huge datasets to create generative, sometimes immersive artworks, or like [Anna Ridler](https://annaridler.com/) experimenting with small datasets, or [Sasha Stiles](https://aifutures.substack.com/p/interview-with-ai-artist-and-poet) with a very data\-forward visual style that presents poetry written by her AI counterpart.

That was a quick and WAY simplistic drive\-by of the subject \- apologies to the thousands of artists who I love and didn’t mention \- but the real reason to abbreviate is that I came here to talk about diamonds.

Guys, they’ve discovered how to [store data in diamonds](https://www.newscientist.com/article/2457948-record-breaking-diamond-storage-can-save-data-for-millions-of-years/) and it’s a LOT \- 1\.85 terabytes per cubic centimeter. They did this by using lasers to displace some of the carbon atoms in a diamond and then stuffing data into the gaps. A stuffed diamond will last millions of years. And it’s so pretty!

It got me thinking about jewelery and what you could make that is both jewelery and information.
