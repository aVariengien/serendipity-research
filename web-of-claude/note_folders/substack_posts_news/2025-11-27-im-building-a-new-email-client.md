I have finally made up my mind about how I want the email app to work. In the spirit of ship early, ship frequently, I’m going to release a public beta version.

## Why build a new email client?

Have you ever noticed that most of your emails these days are not from people anymore? They are mostly automated updates, notifications, promotions, or OTP from companies and services.

The way we use email has changed, but the email clients we use have not.

I’m obsessed with productivity. It’s the same type of obsession that made me create DevUtils, TypingMind, Xnapper, BlackMagic, basically all of my products. I always want to squeeze the last few seconds I can from everything I do, and I use emails every day.

I feel like there is a lot I can do to make my day-to-day interaction with emails more efficient, especially in the AI era.

That’s why I’m building this new email client. I’ll call it “Midday”. This is what the latest prototype looks like:

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!s47U!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F74505f2f-575b-48fa-ae33-cef2be2b4890_2225x1383.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!s47U!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F74505f2f-575b-48fa-ae33-cef2be2b4890_2225x1383.png)

I plan to release the first public beta sometime in December.

It doesn’t have all the features yet, so if you can’t replace all of your email usage with Midday. For example, I’m currently using both Midday and my previous email client in parallel.
