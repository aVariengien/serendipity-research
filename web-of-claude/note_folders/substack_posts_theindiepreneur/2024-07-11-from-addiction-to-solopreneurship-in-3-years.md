Let me explain.

# How reading books affected me

Look, when you play games every free moment that you have, you grow to think that there is nothing else that could be as fun as playing games.

**It’s an addiction.**

In 2020, at age 26, I serendipitously laid my hands on a book that completely changed my life. It was the first self\-help book I have ever read.

Looking back, the author gave very dubious suggestions to be better, like sleeping less than 7 hours every day (Which I tried for some time)

But although they were bad, the book gave me a purpose different than reaching the Top 500 tier in Overwatch.

See, reading a book cover to cover was something I never thought I’d do. If you asked me about it, I’d give you the generic response of: “I am not a books person. I don’t enjoy it”. And to be honest, 99% of the people who say that never gave it a chance.

After reading a book and enjoying most of it, I moved on to another book on the subject and learned about saving money, which sent me on a trajectory of learning how to make money (The book was Rich Dad Poor Dad) and investing actively in stocks.

## First turning point \- quitting games

Now this is a whole other story for another article. But I’ll try to make it short.

I tried to quit games. More times and methods found online than I can count. That wasn’t the problem though. It wasn’t what I tried, but what I didn’t have.

According to Charles Duhigg, the author of The Power of Habit, the best way to replace a habit is not to go cold turkey and stop it. It’s finding something else to do when the trigger to perform the old habit arises.

So for me, when I tried to quit, I didn’t have anything else to do instead. So I fell back.

When I had a new purpose, which was saving money and investing in stocks, I suddenly had a lot of things to do instead of playing games. February 2021 was the first time I said no to playing video games in order to read financial reports, which marked the end of my gaming addiction.

## Second turning point \- quitting my job

It takes one book to change your life. And I have experienced it twice.

The second time was after I read MJ DeMarco’s book, The Millionaire Fastlane.

In the book, DeMarco shares his life story and explains why penny\-pinching and investing in stocks is not a good strategy to live by. And it hit extremely hard because I spent the previous 2\.5 years doing nothing but that.

2 Weeks later, I took the step I wanted to for the previous 3 years, and quit my job.

## Final words

Now, these changes would never happen if I didn’t read books. And if I started my journey with MJ’s books, I probably wouldn't be where I am today.

Probably because I didn’t have any money back then or any knowledge besides playing games to become a full\-time disciplined solopreneur, so it wouldn’t impact me as much.

The past year and a half were the most impactful in terms of reading books for me. In a future article, I will explain how to read books effectively and why you should start reading if you aren’t already.
