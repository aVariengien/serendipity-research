# The secrets banks hide from you

*And why it might ruin your financial life.*

Published: 2024-02-12
Source: https://theindiepreneur.substack.com/p/the-stock-market-will-ruin-your-financial

---

Do you have some money lying around? Well, then you probably heard about the stock market and its endless possibilities to make you rich and retire happily. Right? Wrong!

The first thing you need to consider next time you hear “Pay yourself first. Invest 10% of your salary in the S\&P500 and let your money grow.” Or “The compound effect”.  
Before I tell you why both of these will possibly change your financial life, and not for the good, let’s take an example, shall we?

#### An optimistic example

The average salary in Israel is \~13,000 ILS, which is $3,500\.  
The average return of the S\&P in the last 100 years is 7\.54%, adjusted for inflation. Let’s round up to 8%  
Now, assuming you are an engineer and you make more like 10,000$ a year and you put 10% before taxes into the stock market. This is where you’ll be in 30 years

**$1,408,550\.59 in 30 years.** Sounds fantastic, right? A small monthly price for a bright future. Piece of cake.

#### **Here’s why it’s not that easy:**

1. You rely on the fact that the stock market will keep going up for the next 30 years because it did so in the past.
2. You bank on the fact that there won’t be any major crash right before you’re about to pull out your money.
3. **This one is the most critical.** You HOPE that you will be able to stomach big crashes, that are bound to happen, and keep investing, rather than pulling out your decades of work and religious investing.

Why is this the most critical one? Because the rest are not in your control.  
Watching **$400,000 (30%)** of your hard\-earned money **evaporate** in 30 days is very hard to stomach. And that’s exactly what happened in March of 2020 during COVID.  
  
The harder thing is not knowing whether things will ever get back to being normal and if the stock market will go up again. Whether you’ll still have a job due to mass layoffs or not. Too many unknowns.

The average age of my readers is around 26\. So, assuming it takes you 30 years to gather $1\.4M, you will be at 56\. Ignoring the fact that you might feel too old to use the money the way you wanted when you were young, can you live off of $1\.4M?

Again, the average spend per household in Israel is around $40,000 every year. So, at the last stage of your life, you’ll want to have a good life and use the money, so it’ll be $50,000 for you. That’s just enough for 30 years.

**Keep in mind!** All of that is assuming all the stars align and everything goes as planned, which never is.

### So how should I use my money?

I love Alex Hormozi’s index. It’s called S\&ME.  
Invest in yourself first. Buy things that will make you a better person, a better partner, more productive and smarter than yesterday. How? Plenty of options.  
Books, courses, businesses, education, mentors.

Heck, use the money to pay freelancers to make your dream idea come true. It’s so easy to find people through Upwork or Fiverr. With $1,000 per month, you can accomplish so much!

And the cool thing about it? It stays with you. All the knowledge, experience and lessons are yours to keep and grow.   
When you invest in the stock market and you lose your money, you not only lose years of hard work, but **you also get nothing out of it.**

Keep that in mind and ask the next person who suggests you invest in the stock market these hard questions.
