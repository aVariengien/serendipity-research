# I made a game for Taylor Swift

*And I want you to play it!*

Published: 2024-11-07
Source: https://theindiepreneur.substack.com/p/i-made-a-game-for-taylor-swift

---

It’s 2023\. Taylor Swift publishes her tour dates along with instructions to how to buy tickets to her concert:

1. Choose one country that you want to see the show at.
2. Sign up to \[Find the name of the website]
3. Join the raffle
4. Wait for an email that will tell you whether you won the raffle or not.

What do the winners get?

A chance to buy a ticket by being randomized into a queue of early tickets purchase. Along with another hundreds of thousands.

The day has arrived.

I am in the office, working for a startup. Warm and sunny outside, cold and air\-conditioned inside. Apparently there are more swifties in the office that want to get a ticket to the show

We open TicketMaster, get in queue as soon as the lines open and wait to get our position in the queue for both cities.

Paris \- over 100,000\.  
Germany \- over 60,000\.

When our turn finally arrived, an hour later, we had no tickets left.

That was depressing. A show that we’ve been waiting for, for over a year and we the tickets were sold out.

Out of nowhere, a friend of my sister tells her that he has a good position in the queue and that there’s a chance to get them!

We logged into his TicketMaster account and saw that there are VIP tickets. 430 euro each. In Amsterdam.

At the heat of the moment, we snagged three tickets and promised ourselves a place on the floor, as close to the stage as it gets.

---

Fast forward to April ‘24, my sister and I came up with an idea.

Since we’re part of the few hundreds who get to be on the floor and fight for the chance to be as close as possible to Taylor Swift, we need to act accordingly and show up to stand in line early.

That’s when my sister and I thought:

Let’s build an app to pass the time in the line (Which starts at 6:00 am when the show is scheduled to start at 7:30pm), make it Taylor themed and put a few advertisements in it.

That way we might be able to cover the expenses of the show and the tickets.

## The first attempt

That way we can both have a good time in the line and make a few bucks to cover the expenses of the trip.

We had 3 different games, all trivia based.

Our plan was this:

1. Make the app
2. Advertise it creatively in line
3. Make some money from advertisement.

Fast forward to the day of the show.

8:00am, July. Comfortable, yet chilly weather. We are sitting outside the stadium in Amsterdam (Johan Cruijff Arena), waiting with dozens other of swifties to fight over the chance to stand as close as possible to Taylor Swift (In the end I’ll tell you where we ended up).

Some of them make friendship bracelets, some play card games. We were on the bracelets team.

As the time passes, the pressure grows inside to start telling people about the new application and get people to use it.

It’s that feeling of sressfull butterflies in the stomach, when you know you need to do something, but you just can’t. And it’s the feeling of letting down my sister as well, so it’s multiplied.

Suddenly at 2:00PM, people from the back of the sitting line stand up and start moving towards the front, to get as close as possible to the gates.

This triggers a chain events that cause us to stand for an hour and half, squeezed between other swifties.

This was the moment I knew. We’ve lost the chance to let others use our weeks of work.

## The second attempt

Days after the concert, I believed that it was over and that I should move on to other projects.

Then my sister suddenly comes up with a suggestion to keep building it. She said that she sees many swifties on Instagram, having Swift\-themed nights, parties and more.

I took me some time to come around, but I agreed on spending 5 hours a week on this side project.

For the next 2 months we worked together to build a different game. Something more social that can be played even if you’re not next to each other.

**The idea this time?**

A Kahoot\-like quiz game, Taylor Swift themed.

### The results

After writing codes for over 60 hours and doing a few hours of test runs, we’ve finally hit a stable version that works.

There are many more things to fix and test. The technical side is pretty challenging and I’ll write in depth about it in an article in the future.

But for now, we created a landing page to see how many people are interested in the game, to assess whether we should keep running with it or not.

If you are into Taylor Swift and want to give the game a spin 👇👇👇

[PlayTaylor](https://www.playtaylor.com)

In the next few weeks we’ll follow closely and see how many interested swifties we have and keep you updated.
