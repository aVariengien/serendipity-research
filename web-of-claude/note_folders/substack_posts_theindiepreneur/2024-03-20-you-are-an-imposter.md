# You are an imposter.

*And here's why it's completely fine.*

Published: 2024-03-20
Source: https://theindiepreneur.substack.com/p/you-are-an-imposter

---

👋 *Hey, it’s [Orel](https://www.linkedin.com/in/orel-zilberman-225a37137/) here! Welcome to my weekly newsletter where I share my journey and lessons as an entrepreneur who quit his job to chase his dreams.*

*I am a software developer, and so far I have x3 failed projects, and x1 ongoing.*

*I am also publishing along with* [Anton Zaides](https://open.substack.com/users/121956618-anton-zaides?utm_source=mentions) tech book summaries and practical tips [here](https://techbooks.substack.com/).

The feeling of doubt can creep into any of us at any time.

“Am I a fraud? Why would people follow me?"  
”People surely see that I am just repeating other’s messages”  
”I need to think of something of my own, otherwise I am just another one of the herd”

And if it did for you, you came to the right article.

### Personal experience

In September 2023, I started posting daily on LinkedIn and publishing a weekly YouTube video.

Why? In the beginning, I wanted to share the knowledge I gained and see where it leads me. Just another challenge.   
As time went by I realized that I did something good. I started building a community.

At one point, after [Anton Zaides](https://open.substack.com/users/121956618-anton-zaides?utm_source=mentions) shared my name on his post, things really started moving for me (I got like 70 followers in one day, which is a big deal for me)

And then I started feeling like my posts were nothing but a regurgitation of other people’s ideas. All I did was look at the notes I wrote after reading books and find a good idea that would resonate with people.  
At times, I just looked for something I could write about.

That’s when I thought that

### Everybody knows it already!

In my mind, people already read all the books that I read and know it all. I am not giving them anything new, so why would they want to follow me?

Apparently, this phenomenon has a name. It’s called the “Curse of Knowledge”.   
it is a cognitive bias where we incorrectly assume that everyone knows as much as we do on a given topic.

Anyway, with a heavy heart, I kept on posting in the morning and forgot about it later that day.

Though, most days I would think to myself:

### You will be exposed soon

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!_nRt!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F24487656-346e-46f9-9bea-34521ab20c14_318x317.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!_nRt!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F24487656-346e-46f9-9bea-34521ab20c14_318x317.png)

Someone will surely figure out that you are repeating this message from David Goggins's book and will write about it in the comments and then everybody will hate you.

This is called The “Spotlight effect”. Let me get Wiki to help me with the definition.

The **spotlight effect** is the psychological phenomenon by which people tend to believe that they are being noticed more than they really are. Being that one is constantly in the center of one’s own world, an *accurate* evaluation of how much one is noticed by others is uncommon.

The truth for me back then was: “Nobody gives a damn about what you write. Most of your followers are random people you sent connection requests to”.

And to be honest, a small percentage of your audience cares enough to read, a smaller percentage to react to it and a minuscule percentage cares enough to write a comment.

Alex Hormozi, the author of 100M Leads and 100M Offers, once said:

“You’re going to die. Two weeks after you die most people will have forgotten about you. 6 months after you die no one will talk about you.

 If people don’t care about you in the most important event of your life, when you stop living, how much credibility or weight should we give people about what we do with our lives?”

Although very few people did care, over time I have come to realize that

### I am an imposter.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!dNMI!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2e18e366-5840-4f70-a19e-d233569378cb_450x450.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!dNMI!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2e18e366-5840-4f70-a19e-d233569378cb_450x450.png)

This was a huge relief and revelation for me. I could finally be real with myself.  
But what I also realized is that it’s completely fine. And that I am definitely not the only one.

The coin dropped after I watched Mark Manson’s YouTube video where he said this:

“… Since my job is to read stuff all day and then regurgitate it to people with poop jokes and f\-bombs…”

That sentence right there changed my whole perspective towards writing online and sharing.

### What’s an imposter syndrome?

This is from Wiki:

**Impostor syndrome**, also known as **impostor phenomenon** or **impostorism**, is a psychological occurrence in which people doubt their skills, talents, or accomplishments and have a persistent internalized fear of being exposed as frauds.

For me, it came in the form of feeling like a fraud. Like I copy other’s work for my own benefit. And I did!

Let me tell you now

### Why it’s completely fine to copy

When you choose to share ideas that you got from a book, article or any other source of information, besides reiterating already known knowledge, you also:

#### 1\. Spread the idea

Not everybody has access to your information and very few people read as many books as you do.

When you post something that resonates with you, you share that knowledge with people, who might’ve never read this anywhere.

So you are doing a service to yourself, the source of your knowledge by spreading his message and to your community by sharing valuable information.

#### 2\. Share your beliefs and points of view

This is how people can know about your content if they can relate to it.

When you write what interests you and what you think about it, in essence you share your thoughts and reveal who you are.

#### 3\. Share your unique experience

And this is the magic sauce. That’s what truly sets you apart from others.

People out there might be in the same situation you are in, or that you were in, and help them resonate and perhaps even get value from it for their journey.

Other people will be interested to see how your life unfolds to get inspired and maybe one day try it out themselves.

Some people would just be genuinely interested in what you have to say.

And the rest will not follow you, so it doesn’t matter.

### Final words

It’s okay to copy other’s work. You are special in your own way, with your own experiences and your own set of beliefs.

In my experience, even if there are people who judge you and think you are a fraud, they won’t express their opinion (Besides by not liking the post, maybe), so you are free to believe people just don’t care enough yet.

And that’s also fine. You are probably a beginner and you need to gain credibility. Go out there and share your story.
