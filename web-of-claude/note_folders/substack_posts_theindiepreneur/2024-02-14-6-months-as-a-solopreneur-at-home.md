# 6 Months as a solopreneur at home

*As a software developer who quit his job*

Published: 2024-02-14
Source: https://theindiepreneur.substack.com/p/6-months-as-a-solopreneur-at-home

---

Almost 6 months ago I decided to quit my job and spend time chasing my dreams of building something of my own. Be financially unconstrained. Not “having” to do anything but choose to.

#### The first 2 months \- The hype

During the beginning of my independent life, I decided to start building up my brand and my social presence.  
I published a few YouTube videos and several posts on LinkedIn. I also traveled to Ireland and Croatia for 3 weeks.

Things were going extremely well and I felt like I was rolling. My LinkedIn impressions were growing as well as the subscriber count on YouTube.  
I found a like\-minded friend who’s a bookworm like me ( [Anton Zaides](https://open.substack.com/users/121956618-anton-zaides?utm_source=mentions) )

I started to work with a partner on an application to revolutionize Israeli chess clubs, which was on the brink of its first release and I was developing a mobile game in Unity.

Then, a war broke out.

#### The next month \- The depression

LinkedIn was full of pro\-Israel explanatory content and I felt that if I didn’t have anything war\-beneficial to share, I should not. I felt the same way about YouTube.

Chess clubs around the country closed specifically the one I attended and that my partner was running, so it made no sense back then to keep developing and spending money.

In short \- nothing was going well. I felt like I was stuck in addition to the fact that I was constantly worried and anxious about the war. I’ve read the news more times than I have ever done in my whole life.  
I found it extremely hard to wake up in the morning and be productive \- because I didn’t feel like there was anything to be productive at.'

#### The last 3 months \- A new beginning

I decided to reach out to Anton and see what’s he up to and how is he coping with the situation. He was on parental leave for the next 4 weeks and he had some time on his hands. He was also one of those who encouraged me to get back to posting weekly videos on YouTube, which I did.  
  
after a few messages, I told him that I was trying to work on my game and he told me that he had some ideas for a new project. I was hyped. I thought: “Great! This is my moment to get back in the game and feel good again”.  
And that’s how BookWizard was born.

We decided to create a better version of Goodreads for us to use and for other bookworms like us. We rolled a few ideas and decided to start having a weekly meeting, a Jira board (which was abandoned pretty quickly) and hire someone to design the website for us.

#### Present time \- Rebuilding the brand

Nowadays, after our first closed\-circuit release of the product, we found out that there’s a lot more work to do because the UX was horrible (Note to self: **NEVER** settle on the first person that sounds good enough).

I also decided to take my social presence to the next level and take steps to increase my presence (By posting daily for 30 days \+ newsletters), responding to people’s posts and videos on YouTube, focusing on one specific niche rather than talking about whatever I find interesting and generally following others’ advice, especially Anton’s document that he made for me.

So that was a summary of my first half a year as an out\-of\-work entrepreneur. There are highs and lows and I am sure that the worst and the best are yet to come!
