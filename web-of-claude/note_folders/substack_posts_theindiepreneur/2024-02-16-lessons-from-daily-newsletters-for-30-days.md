# Lessons from daily newsletters for 30 days

*Hint: I didn't complete the challenge.*

Published: 2024-02-16
Source: https://theindiepreneur.substack.com/p/lessons-from-daily-newsletters-for

---

A few days ago I decided to 10X my efforts to increase my social presence and grow a community.  
After a few discussions with [Anton Zaides](https://open.substack.com/users/121956618-anton-zaides?utm_source=mentions), I figured out that that might be the wrong way to 10X at this moment.

Don’t get me wrong, posting daily will probably make me a better writer and my community will grow.

But at a much slower rate than if I posted daily on LinkedIn and maybe twice a week diving deep into the struggles and successes of a guy who decided to chase his dreams and quit his comfortable 6 figures salary job.

#### Why the shift? 🔄

1. **I can spend the time commenting and getting to know people** 🗣️  
I want people to see my newsletters and get value out of them. If I grow a community in between each newsletter, I increase the chances to reach more people with each newsletter.
2. **The “I will run out of ideas” fear 😨**  
To be honest, it’s scary. What if one day I have no idea for something new and original? I mean, I have like 15 ideas in the backlog and I feel like that’s all I’ve got. So, if I post twice a week, I have more time to gather more ideas and not become repetitive and boring.
3. **Not enough happens in my life for me to publish daily 💻**I mean, I stay at home and write/program all day. And when I don’t, I spend time with my girlfriend/read. So I have my YouTube channel for extras that I want to share, the TechBook substack for interesting books related to tech to share more. I want to grow this community around entrepreneurship.

#### The new game plan 📝

* **Two Newsletters a Week**: By lowering the rate of publishing I will improve the quality of each newsletter.
* **Daily LinkedIn Posts**: I will post daily lessons, inspiration or events that happened that are related to my journey.
* **Daily Engagement**: Daily commenting and communication with like\-minded people or people who are interested in what I have to say :)

I could see this as a failure to achieve my 30\-day goal. And to be honest, I have contemplated this move for some time and felt like a failure if I didn’t at least finish it.  
But, eventually, I chose see it as a lesson from which I learned:

**One**: Not to be too hasty [😅](https://emojipedia.org/grinning-face-with-sweat)  
**Two:** Focus on my niche and not try to talk about everything that interests me🔍

Thank you for reading my newsletter! Join the indiepreneurs community for more updates and lessons!
