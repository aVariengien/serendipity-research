When you start your entrepreneurship journey, everything is an unknown.

* What you’ll write
* What you’ll work on
* Will you make money
* Is it even worth the trouble
* Is it okay to have days where you do nothing?

And because everything is an unknown, it’s also overwhelming. It feels like you **HAVE** to start showing some results otherwise why are you here and not getting your salary, working 5 days a week?

In today’s article, I am going to share my way of dealing with unknowns and pushing forward.

## How to deal with idleness

When you feel like doing nothing, it’s most likely due to burnout.

I am not an expert on the subject, but I’ve been through several and I am going through one as I write this article.

After spending 100\+ hours in 10 days to build my first solo startup, I feel completely out of energy. And I launched it 9 days ago.

I have shared [my way of regaining your motivation](https://theindiepreneur.substack.com/p/how-to-regain-your-motivation-as?r=ug4y7), and most times, that’s not enough.

It’s one thing to feel better about having a burnout and to slowly regain motivation. **But you also have to take actions and start getting back the momentum you had before.**

## Why taking action is important

Look, taking your time to think and contemplate things is important. But most times these thoughts become overthinking, which can easily lead you down a rabbit hole of negative thoughts and self\-doubt.

I’ve been there. Right before I started writing this article.

These thoughts undermotivate you. They make you want to stop and just go back to the comfort of having something safe.

Once you start taking action, different kind of thoughts appear. You start thinking about things that you read or heard that helped you earlier to gain motivation.

> **Full disclosure:**   
> I have just watched a YouTube video of Alex Hormozi talking about burnout to try to get a bit of boost to finish the article.
> 
> In the video, Alex talked about distancing ourselves from our emotions and thoughts.
> 
> We might feel stressed or burnt out, and we should acknowledge those feelings.  
> But we shouldn’t let them control our behaviour and actions.

Now in order to take action, you need to know which actions to take.

And please, do not choose what to do on the fly.

## Plan ahead

Decide what you’re going to accomplish the night before. Especially when you feel burnt out.

Setting daily goals and even planning exactly when you’re going to work on each of those will take off the heavy weight of deciding what you need to do next.

When you don’t feel like doing anything, you know everything you should do. You just don’t want to. And having to choose between a million different things when you feel stressed out at the beginning of the day is almost impossible.

**Why is it easier the night before?** Because in the morning not only you don’t know what you should start working on, you know that you need to be productive and you have just started your day.

You put yourself in an extra stressful environment to make the decision of what to do next.

The night before you feel more free, because your day is done and you don’t have any pressure to be productive. You just need to brush your teeth and go to bed.

So at this point you are starting to overcome your idleness feelings and you take actions that refill your motivation tank.

For me, I still have those emotions and feelings deep inside. I am a known caver.  
I bury my feelings deep inside to avoid the bad feeling of confronting them.

## Talk to someone

3 months ago I started talking to a psychologist. For different reasons though.

As a caver, most feelings are just saved somewhere in the back of your mind just to pop up later and enhance any emotions, mostly those that cause you an unpleasant feeling in the body.

You feel anxious and stressed about very small things, like sharing your feelings and saying what’s on your mind to someone who cares about you.

After going through 8 sessions, I feel that I am more open to my shrink and to the people in my life. I share much more than I did before and the emotions are not as overwhelming as they were.

Being able to share what you feel helps relieve most of the pain that’s caused by overloading your emotional system and get out of the rabbit hole of thoughts faster.

Give it a go. :)

## Final words

The life of the starting entrepreneur is a cycle of highs and lows, which looks like a sinus wave.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vXYj!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6acc07d1-61f1-4154-8a67-676e805ca52e_629x364.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vXYj!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6acc07d1-61f1-4154-8a67-676e805ca52e_629x364.png)

Our goal is to learn how to recognize where we stand in the graph and act accordingly to keep moving forward and to avoid losing all the we’ve achieved so far.

I personally see the change over time and the ability to recognize my position better and I learned how to handle those times.

But it’s still hard and the thoughts of stopping everything and just going back to the old way will creep in on you. Quite a lot.

Does it mean that you need to do it? I don’t think so.   
It’s the fear of “Are you doing it all for nothing” that reveals its head to test you.
