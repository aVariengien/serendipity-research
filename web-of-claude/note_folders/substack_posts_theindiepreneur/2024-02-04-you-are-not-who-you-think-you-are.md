# You are not who you think you are

*But you can change it*

Published: 2024-02-04
Source: https://theindiepreneur.substack.com/p/you-are-not-who-you-think-you-are

---

We’ve grown to believe that our childhood and society determine our personality. We have to play with the hand we’re dealt and there’s nothing we can do about it.  
  
There are even personality tests to verify your beliefs about yourself and give you an exact description of You.  
Let’s see for example the DISC. We’ll use Thomas Erikson’s colors and descriptions from his book Surrounded by Idiots.

**Red (Dominance, D):** The Dominant Red personality is characterized by assertiveness, directness, and a focus on achieving results. Reds are often strong\-willed, determined, and competitive. They prefer to take charge and are action\-oriented individuals.

**Yellow (Influence, I):** The Influencing Yellow personality is outgoing, social, and enthusiastic. Yellows are natural communicators and enjoy interacting with others. They are creative, expressive, and persuasive individuals who thrive in social settings.

**Green (Steadiness, S):** The Steady Green personality is known for being supportive, reliable, and empathetic. Greens are good listeners and team players. They prioritize harmonious relationships and seek to maintain a peaceful and stable environment.

**Blue (Conscientiousness, C):** The Compliant Blue personality is analytical, detail\-oriented, and structured. Blues are often systematic, organized and strive for accuracy. They value precision and follow rules and guidelines carefully.

According to Erikson and the DISC assessment, each of us has 1\-3 colors that build our personality and character. Erikson also gives us examples of each character and describes possible combinations and how to deal with each combination. He also foretells which match will be good in a romantic partnership and which not.

I mean, people never change, right..? wrong.

Although it might sound nice to have an excuse of why we’re not chasing our dreams because “We’re not built for it. It’s not in our DNA”, these are excuses.  
Zig Ziglar, a well\-known motivational speaker and author, famously said:

> *"You are what you are and you are where you are because of what has gone into your mind. You can change what you are and you can change where you are by changing what goes into your mind."*

According to neuroscience studies, your brain works on the “use it or lose it” policy.  
See, you have neurons in your brain that communicate at synapses. That’s a path. The more you take that path, the stronger the connection is made. The less you take it, the weaker it becomes.

#### I used to be a Green. A strong Green.

That kind that would say anything to make you happy. I would lie to be accepted and loved. I would say what you want to hear to avoid a confrontation. often at the expense of my happiness.  
  
Even though I might’ve disagreed with you, I would externally agree or change the subject to avoid hard feelings. If I “accidentally” expressed my opinion and you would receive it badly, I would overthink it for at least an hour.

“Maybe they don’t like me anymore”   
”Am I good enough for this friendship?”  
”Perhaps I am a bad person and I should just accept it and live the villain’s life”  
  
**In short**? I cared too much what you think of me.

A couple of years ago I decided I don’t want that anymore. There’s no real reason to avoid confrontations when I put myself first. So, I started noticing these moments when I had that urge to satisfy others and sometimes even lie to look good, and forced myself, when I noticed soon enough, to change the behavior. To do the opposite of what I had the instinct to do.

99% Of the times I failed miserably. But that 99% slowly turned into 98%, then 90% and today I believe that I’m around 10%. It took some effort, some youtube videos and dozens of books, but I am progressing.

#### My point? **Your personality is not permanent!**

All these personality assessments are nice and are, unfortunately, permanent for the people who believe them.But you can change it. If there’s a certain thing you don’t like about your personality, change it! Work on it!

#### Here are 3 easy ways that you can start with today:

1. Read books on the topic. Here are 3 that you can start with:

	1. Mindset \- Carol Dweck
	2. Soundtracks \- Jon Acuff
	3. Personality Isn’t Permanent \- Benjamin Hardy
2. To help you notice the feelings and thoughts before you act on them, try to practice mindfulness meditation. The practice will help you be more aware of the present moment and your feelings and thoughts.
3. Ask your partner/family member/close friend to help you. Share with them what you want to change and tell them to let you know whenever you do that action.

You are not shackled by your “predetermined personality”. Become who you want to become, and start now.
