## Tiny bets (Products I built)

In November I was quite productive with the tiny bets. I had two products I published, had a video editor create a beautiful video for one of them and ran a Google ads with it.

#### Build Quick

I built a product that helps developers get from idea to deploy in a few minutes.

I used v0\.dev to generate beautiful landing pages, from which user can choose the one they like the most.

Then, they describe their idea for a product, and then, using openai API, I tailor this landing page to their idea, along with an email input, so they can validate their idea extremely fast.

I was sure this one was going to be a banger. It’s a very strong painkiller.

After paying hundreds of dollars on a video and Google ads, the results are:

1 sign up. And it was a troll.

#### StripeGuard (Not official yet)

After reading [Greg Isenberg](https://open.substack.com/users/431713-greg-isenberg?utm_source=mentions)’s twit, where he suggested a few dozens of ideas, this one caught my interest.

A Stripe webhook monitor, which notifies about failed webhooks.

The first thing that went through my mind is: *“How come nobody has thought about it already?”*

Then I started implementing it. Much more complicated than it seems.

After 3 long conversations with Stripe support and 5 days of non\-stop coding, I managed to come up with an algorithm to figure out when a webhook has failed and notify the owner.

Now, the only problem I have left is Stripe is not available in my country, so I can’t use production for it.

Luckily, there’s a solution! **Stripe Atlas.**

I paid Stripe $500 for a service in which they create an an LLC company in the US, through which I can charge money using Stripe services.

I am currently waiting for an EIN.

## One habit that I picked

For the past few weeks I decided to try and focus on one thing at a time.

That means that every week, besides writing online and doing some freelance work, I focus on one product or an idea, and see it to it’s end.

I used to work on multiple projects in parallel, which was super ineffective and gave me a feeling of being stuck.

Now that I finish whatever I start, be it a success or a failure, I can say wholeheartedly that it’s done.

And the results speak for themselves.

## One book I enjoyed reading

Discourses on Livy, By Niccolo Machiavelli.

During a detox weekend I decided to start reading some history, and since I enjoyed The Prince (Also by Machiavelli), I read his other book.

And wow, it was incredibly interesting!

Machiavelli describes how societies rise and decline, and gives his remarks about leadership, power and human nature.

I don’t want to spoil it for you, but the things he says feel extremely relevant to today’s society.
