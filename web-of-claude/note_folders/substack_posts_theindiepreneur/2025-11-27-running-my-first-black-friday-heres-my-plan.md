# Running My First Black Friday. Here's My Plan.

Published: 2025-11-27
Source: https://theindiepreneur.substack.com/p/my-first-ever-black-friday

---

Every year I see how successful Black Friday is.

People wait for an entire year just to get their hands on a juicy discount on their brand new TV, waiting all night in line.

I was never that type of person.

If I need something, I get it. I talk to someone in the store, ask for their honest opinion, which they usually give, and purchase whatever they said. And now, when I have a valuable product, I considered running a Black Friday of my own.

But this stuff works. Every single year.

## **My problem with Black Friday**

Every time I thought about doing it for WriteStack, I hit the brakes.

**1\. Will it make WriteStack look cheap?**

Probably. And that’s the opposite of what I’m building.

**2\. What will current users think?**

If they paid full price and now see 60% off… that’s a slap in the face.

And these are legit concerns.

When you see something for 70% off, you suddenly think that it’s junk and someone wants to get rid of it ASAP.

And, in my mind at least, when users see that you suddenly offer a 60% discount on something they paid full price for, you train them to wait for your discounts instead of purchasing on the spot.

But I was thinking about it completely wrong.

After reading Tibo’s newsletter (the creator of Taplio, Tweethunter and many more awesome products), I understood something.

## The other side of Black Friday

When done correctly, Black Friday does something amazing for a SaaS product.

1. Lowers the barrier for people on the fence
2. Creates urgency without desperation
3. Maintains product value and positioning

But you gotta keep the discount meaningful, not stupid. You remove friction, not giving it away for free.

A big creator suggested I do 50% for an entire year. But it feels like I am almost giving it for free.

I mean, WriteStack is not meant to be a cheap product that you get at a discount on AppSumo (*guilty for trying it once 🙈*).

It’s a premium tool that automates your entire Notes workflow.

That’s exactly why attracting ‘discount hunters’ is the LAST thing I want. So I found a smarter way to do Black Friday.

## Here’s how I do it

Instead of getting you to pay for something you might not use, I’ll do you and I one better.

**40% off for your first month.**

Why only one month? **Because if in 30 days you didn’t get anything, the discount isn’t the problem.**

I am not trying to trick people into buying something that they won’t use. I don’t want to make people waste money.

I want to remove friction for those people who considered it but weren’t sure yet if it’s for them.

So you get 40% off for the first month. Love it? Keep it. If not, cancel. You’re not locked into some ‘50% off for a year’ shenanigans like other products do.
