# Being lonely at home as an indiepreneur

*And how to deal with it*

Published: 2024-02-15
Source: https://theindiepreneur.substack.com/p/being-lonely-at-home-as-an-indiepreneur

---

Being lonely doesn’t always mean being alone. Because I am not.  
I am living with my supportive girlfriend and my sister is also very supportive of me.  
But not everybody can understand the struggle of being bored and stressed because you have nothing to do yet you need to “justify” the fact that you quit your job and have 0 income.

They sure are there and you can talk to them and share your struggles, but you are not looking for an ear (at least I didn’t). You are looking for new ways to kickstart your motivation again. You want to find out how to solve the boredom problem.

### Key takeaway

Always have a friend you appreciate on speed dial (Though make sure to foster your relationship and not just send a message when in need).

And I am not talking about a friend you go out with or you share your deepest stories with, which can be helpful too, but wasn’t for me.  
I am talking about someone who is driven just like you and who does things you admire to make his future easier and better.

### How it all started

For those of you who are new here, first of all, welcome! :)  
My name is Orel and I am a software engineer. On August 23 I decided that I didn’t want to keep working for the company I did back then, which completely aligned with my goals to become an entrepreneur, build things of my own and be financially free. I have elaborated more in [another newsletter](https://updateyourlife.substack.com/publish/posts/detail/141632797?referrer=%2Fpublish%2Fposts) of mine, so feel free to go check it out!

#### What feeling lonely means to me

It means having little to nothing to do and having no one around me to truly understand my pain.  
It’s coping with the boredom and the fact that you need to “justify” your job quitting and the fact that you have 0 income.   
It’s living in the present without having a clue about what you need to do next, and if you do have a clue, you have no idea how and when.

It’s contemplating whether you made a mistake and you should go back to work, or whether it’s a part of everybody’s journey.  
It’s trying to find the motivation to do something, even going out to the gym to try to get a new idea or a fresh view of life.

#### How I managed to overcome a very rough period

So before the war broke out in my country, I was posting daily on LinkedIn and I was starting to get traction. I was on a roll.  
And during the war, my feed was filled with pro\-Israel explanatory posts and I felt like it would be dishonoring and not respectful to write something different than that.

At one point I decided that I was going to try and find someone who could understand me and perhaps I could learn from how to deal with the then\-current situation. I sent a message to [Anton Zaides](https://open.substack.com/users/121956618-anton-zaides?utm_source=mentions) and after a few back and forth and the fact that he was on parental leave, we decided to start a project together.

That was the **turning point** for me.

#### The lesson from that rough phase

**Always keep in contact with people you appreciate.**Talking to these people, who will eventually become your friends, is crucial to give yourself a boost of courage and motivation to keep going.   
You get to share your bad times and good times, figure out how to work through them and come out better and you might stumble upon great opportunities for cooperation!
