## Tiny bets (Products I built)

### WriteRoom

Ever since I started my journey, I tried to build many products, usually multiple at once, and use the 'ship and see what sticks' method.

Needless to say, it has failed miserably.

So, in the past 2 months I decided to focus on one product and one product only and for more than a week after launch.

#### What is it?

That product is called WriteRoom (Previously Article Generate).

It's a suite of tools for Substack writers, who look to grow and get more out of their Substack without sacrificing hours everyday.

It started last month as an AI\-powered text editor, to help you come up with ideas and outlines for your articles and avoid staring at a blank page.

It turned into a note generator, a wall of inspiration (for notes) and a place where you can organize all your notes.

#### How do I market it?

Well, marketing it has been rough, to say the least.

I am trying to build warm connection with interesting people, by commenting on Substack notes, articles and sending DMs.

Then, if the person I am talking with shows interest, I would present them with the product and let them use it for free.

If the person I am talking to shows no interest? It's okay. I contact interesting people, so I enjoy our conversations.

Most of them are nice enough to give me feedback and tell me what works, what doesn't and what features they'd want to see.

Some just ignore me (Looking at you, [Michał Poczwardowski](https://open.substack.com/users/141222242-micha-poczwardowski?utm_source=mentions) :P).

#### Results?

So far I have 7 active users.

Around 12 that said they'd give it a go, so I am waiting for them, pinging them every once in a while.

Cost: Around $100 so far.

Luckily, I got $1,000 from AWS as credits and I have $5,000 more from Stripe Atlas :).

## One habit I picked

I started meditating every day for 5 minutes.

I gave up meditating some time ago, and I didn’t pick it up because of ego.

I thought to myself: “How can I meditate for 5 minutes every day? I used to mediate 20 minutes. 5 minutes is not worth it”.

The lesson: It’s better to start again, small, then not start at all.
