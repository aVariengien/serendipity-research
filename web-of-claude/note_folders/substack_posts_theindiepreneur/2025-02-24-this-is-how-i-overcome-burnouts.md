## The mental health challenge

### **Personal story of hitting burnout**

When I started my journey, my first solo project was PinkyPartner.

An accountability app in which you create a contract with a friend to build good habits together.

It was back in January 24\.

I was so excited by it and by the fact that I found this incredible name and an incredible domain, that I just want to build it 24/7\.

And that's almost what I did.

I spent 10\-12 hours building PinkyPartner for 2 weeks straight (That was before V0 and Bolt).

After I was done and had published it on ProductHunt, I felt empty.

Weird thoughts of doubt started creeping in and making me think about the decision I took and why did I even do it.

And the worst thing, I felt like I was alone. Nobody can understand me.

For 8 weeks I didn't feel like doing anything or building anything. I was extremely depressed and under motivated.

I was burnt out. Badly.

Luckily, I found ways to overcome the burn outs and they happened to me less and less ever since.

**I have found ways to avoid these burnouts.**

## Practical strategies

### Write your next step

Let me tell you something.

**Having the next step is the fastest cure to burnouts.**

In so many occasions in my journey I felt like I have things to do, but everything was just a mess in mind.

All I wanted to do was procrastinate it and hope it would pass.

Well, 99% of the time it happened, it didn't just go away.

But one thing cured it and filled me with motivation. **Making a list of what I needed to do cured it immediately.**

Give it a go. You'll thank me later.

### Step away once a day

Imagine your mentality as a tightrope walker.

The moment you lose focus and balance, you'll fall. And let me tell you something, you'll fall. Many times.

You need to find a way to keep yourself balanced and that takes practice.

One practical tip that I can give you is stepping away from work at least once a day.

For me it's going to the gym.

Although 80% of the time I don't feel like going to the gym, I do it anyway. It keeps my hormones in the right balance.

### Regain your discipline

Another cause of burnouts is losing discipline and falling back to old, bad habits.

And it doesn't happen at once. It's a slow progress.

Luckily, reverting it isn't as slow. The moment you notice you're losing your discipline, start doing whatever it is that helped you build discipline in the first place.

For me it's reading Can't Hurt Me, by David Goggins.

### Building a support system

#### Share

One of the most important things I found was sharing.

Either people that could understand what I was going through and those who couldn't.

Just putting it out there makes it that much better

#### Find communities

Go on X. Join Build in Public or any other indie hackers group.

Write about your experience and send messages to people who respond to you.

Not only will you find compassion from people who have experienced it, you might just hit an idea for the next thing you can do.

#### Find a partner

Another great motivator and burnout depressor is working with a partner.

When started my journey, the first project I worked on was BookWiz with @Anton Zaides.

Working with someone keeps the loneliness away and forces you to be responsible.

And it's not only in the products you build.

Anton and I used to write together articles in a weekly newsletter about tech books.

Even though I didn't enjoy the topic of the newsletter, I enjoyed writing it. It felt good to put time and effort and write very detailed articles.

## Final words

Going through burnouts is hard. It feels awful.

But there's no reason you'll stay there and not learn how to deal with it, so the next time it happens, it isn't as bad.

I share a burnout checklist, so the next time you go through the cycle you can try everything from my personal toolbox to help you get over it.
