# What I learned from hiring 7 freelancers

*And how I find a good one*

Over the last year, I have completed over 7 contracts with 7 different freelancers for the following jobs:

* YouTube video editing
* Software development
* UX/UI design

### Key takeaways

### Things to expect when hiring a freelancer

#### They start strong and lose steam

Most of the time, freelancers will do the best they can early, so you hire them for more jobs.  
Gradually, the quality of their work or they don’t meet deadlines.

When this starts to happen, you can consider talking to them and figuring out how to move forward.

**How to handle:**

Set clear expectations and let them know that you are not interested in checking their capabilities in the first job.  
You can see them at their best in their portfolio.

#### Good reviews don’t mean a lot

Most people don’t feel comfortable giving someone who did a good job a bad review for something they did badly. Let me explain.

If I hired someone to edit my videos, and the edits were great but the deadlines were not met and the communication was poor.

Then, I’ll rate the communication and adherence to schedule low, which will hurt the overall rating. Here’s an example:

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!akqM!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F682b0420-44bb-4c03-8093-422620937243_125x28.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!akqM!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F682b0420-44bb-4c03-8093-422620937243_125x28.png)

**How to handle:**

Look for the bad reviews to understand what the freelancer is lacking and if you’re okay with it.

#### They mostly underestimate the timeline of completing a job

And that’s okay. Life happens and plans change.  
But if you hire someone for multiple jobs and it happens 2 times or more that you need to push your schedule, move on.

**How to handle:**

From my experience, it usually happens with those who have bad communication and respond less than once in 24 hours.

So if your freelancer takes way too long to respond regularly, find another one that will fit your needs.

### How to know a good one

#### Feedback reception

It’s okay to give feedback. I would consider it highly important.  
Sometimes the product does not fit your vision 100% and some tweaks are required.

Feel free to share that with the freelancer. If the feedback is received and implemented in future jobs, that’s a great sign.

**Pro tip:**A sign of a very good freelancer is not only receiving feedback but also giving out one.

#### Detailed proposal

Many freelancers have a premade proposal that they copy and paste and change minor details to fit your job description.

A good proposal doesn’t include a lot of emojis and is not too flashy.  
It touches all the points of your description and requirements with a personal message.

The better ones also include further questioning and interest in your project.

**Pro tip:**   
Ask the freelancer to add a weird sentence to their proposal. That way you know that they read your proposal.

#### Good communication

When the freelancer is communicative and gives advice, usually that means they’re going to do a good job (According to their experience and your payment).

That way, if something doesn’t go as planned, you can just talk it out and find a solution.

**Pro tip:**If the freelancer cannot join a voice call at all (due to any reason), don’t bother going forward. It only gets worse.

### How to know a bad one

#### The freelancer is a company

There are many companies out there who take the role of “the man in the middle” to play the arbitrage game.

How? They ask you to pay a premium because they get you “the best freelancer” for your job.

It’s bad because usually you don’t get to talk directly to the one who’s going to do the work, and that’s a bad foot to start on. As I wrote earlier, communication is mega important.

#### Deadlines are not met regularly

If the deadline is missed once, that’s okay. Sometimes unexpected things happen and you should let it go. Note it though.

If it happens more than once, without any logical explanation, it’s not a good sign. I would start looking for a replacement.  
You can be more certain about it when the freelancer is also not very good at communication.

#### Response time is high

Usually, when it takes the freelancer more than 24 hours to respond, regularly, it either means that something’s going on in their life or your job is not important enough for them.

And from my experience, it only gets worse. One of the people I hired completely stopped responding at one point.

Remember that many talented freelancers are willing to get the gig and do the best job they can.  
Don’t settle on someone because their products are good or because they’re communicative.

**Make sure to find someone whose bad qualities are not deal\-breakers for you.**
