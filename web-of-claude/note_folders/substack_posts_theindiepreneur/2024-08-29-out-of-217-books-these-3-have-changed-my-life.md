## Book \#1 \- The Intelligent Investor by Benjamin Graham

I started reading books during the COVID stock market book, around August 2020\.

And stock market investing was all anybody talked about in my office. And since I was getting into books, I wanted to see what investing is all about. And anywhere I looked, The Intelligent Investor is what I saw.

Now, It’s not that the book itself has changed something in my life. It’s the fact that I actually managed to read such a long book (640 pages) and did not quit.

It makes you feel empowered.

In fact, it made me feel so empowered that I tried to read “Security Analysis”. This one didn’t end well. Got to page 534 and got too bored to continue.

**Sometimes it’s not the book’s content that changes you. It’s the feeling you get for going through it.**

### How the book changed my life

At the time of reading the book, I was still a video games addict. But I have been trying hard to kick the habit for months now. And after going through The Intelligent Investor I felt so good that I wanted to be an active investor.

And being an active investor takes time. A lot of time.

Learning about businesses, analyzing financial reports and reading dozens of books about investing takes time. And that was the exact routine I needed.

## Book \#2 \- Can’t Hurt Me by David Goggins

I need to get something off my chest:

**I rarely enjoy reading a book.   
90\-95%** of the books that I read, **I read for the knowledge and not for the pleasure.**

And I am okay with it. Beyond learning a lot about topics I am interested in, **it builds discipline**.

Which leads me to David Goggins. The father of all disciplines. The guy who went through Navy SEAL’s hell week three times, got injured in all of them and finished the last one with broken legs and feet.

His story is more than inspiring. It makes you feel weak because deep down you know what he’s talking about and you know that you have never pushed yourself hard enough.

Goggins took it to the extreme of the extreme, but that’s not what he advocates for.

After reading the book, every time you have a lazy thought that tells you to stay in your comfort zone, Goggins will come running, telling you that you’re weak.

And then you’ll find yourself running out in the cold. Just for the sake of it.

So if you’re ready to feel bad every time you get lazy, go ahead and **read the book**. And if you are not ready, well, you need someone to tell you that you’re lazy, so **read the book.**

### How the book changed my life

The thing I wrote that would happen to you is not a guess.   
I know it first hand.

There are too many times in life in which you can choose between staying in your comfort zone or doing the hard things.

And for me, 90% of the time I’m about to do the easy thing, Goggins’ spirit lingers in my thoughts, telling me to do the opposite.

They said in the news that it’s the coldest it has ever been in a few years? **I went running.**

It’s 21:30, I just got home after a long day and all I want is to rest but I didn’t work out? **No matter, I will work out.**

Writing is boring and I don’t feel any immediate satisfaction out of it? **I will spend at least an hour a day writing.**

That voice of “get out of your comfort zone” does not leave me. And it hurts when I disobey it.

In the picture: Me, trying way too hard.

## Book \#3 \- The Millionaire Fastlane by MJ DeMarco

For 3 years I saved every penny I could and invested it into the stock market. Most times I would save 60\-75% of my salary. I also spent hundreds of hours digging into reports and reading about companys’ CEO to figure out what’s a good investment and what’s not.

Until I read The Millionaire Fastlane.

I realized that although I managed to save quite a lot, I was suffering in my jobs. **Every week felt endless**. And the weekends just got me thinking about Sunday morning, when I need to go back to work.

Plus, investing in the stock market will not make me rich. At least not in the foreseeable future (It’ll take 20\-30 years).

DeMarco talks about the 3 lanes in which we walk. Here’s a summary:

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!VfIK!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa9d6c46f-38a3-4649-a493-282fc03580ec_487x339.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!VfIK!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa9d6c46f-38a3-4649-a493-282fc03580ec_487x339.png)

Credit to The Swedish Investor channel on YouTube

I realized that I was walking the slow lane. And I did not want to work and retire at 65, especially because I was suffering.

Plus, nothing guarantees that I will be able to pull my money at 65\. Maybe a stock market crash would take place and I will have to wait? Who knows.

### How the book changed my life

The message hit me so hard that two weeks later I told my team leader that I quit.

I have saved enough money to sustain myself with a $0 income for 3 years. I know I can make an adequate income in 3 years.

I stopped investing in the stock market completely. Whatever I have in there I keep it, and if I need money, I will take it out from my investing accounts.

Instead of saving my money, I try to buy back my time however I can.

And let me tell you. **The freedom is priceless.** Being my own boss, working my own schedule and growing and learning so much about myself is nothing a book or money can give you.

**Here’s my big goal:**  
Be able to spend time with my future family without worrying about bills or not being able to run up the stairs without needing to catch my breath.

And investing in the stock market will not get me there in 10\-15 years.
