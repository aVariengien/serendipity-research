# Top 15 books for entrepreneurs

*Only practical books. No fluff.*

Published: 2025-01-12
Source: https://theindiepreneur.substack.com/p/best-books-for-entrepreneurs

---

“We need to be reminded more than we need to be taught”

Alex Hormozi

For so long I have been reading books just to read them. To tick another box and move on.

This is a fool’s way to go at it.

The better approach is to skim through most books, until you hit a gem. Then, read the hell out of it. Learn it as if you have a test on it.

Get every drop of knowledge juice you can from that book.

This is exactly what I decided to do. I want to improve my marketing skills, so I read only the 5 books you’ll see in the **Marketing** section.

*(The books are not ordered in any particular order)*

## Entrepreneurial

### **1\. The Personal MBA \- Josh Kaufman**

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Kqg4!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F042993a1-d6bd-414a-8c28-4e64469f5486_295x455.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Kqg4!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F042993a1-d6bd-414a-8c28-4e64469f5486_295x455.png)

The Personal MBA \- Josh Kaufman

This book takes the core principles of business and puts them in 436, easy to read pages. It covers everything from sales to systems thinking.

Josh promises you don’t need an MBA. You just need to read the book.

And he delivers.

### **2\. The Mom Test \-** Rob Fitzpatrick

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yQk3!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F17c9a2a7-395d-4e2b-86d1-8bb14f387afb_350x544.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yQk3!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F17c9a2a7-395d-4e2b-86d1-8bb14f387afb_350x544.png)

The Mom Test \- Rob Fitzpatrick

The most practical guide to validating your business ideas.

Rob explains exactly how to ask questions that yield the most honest, actionable insights you can get about your product instead of fluff.

### **3\. Oversubscribed \-** Daniel Priestley

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gU6I!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F719ceef0-390f-46d7-abd8-33cc0675650c_317x493.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gU6I!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F719ceef0-390f-46d7-abd8-33cc0675650c_317x493.png)

Oversubscribed \- Daniel Priestley

If you're tired of chasing customers, this book will show you how to make customers chase you.

Priestley teaches you how to create overwhelming demand for your product by positioning it as something people can’t wait to get.

I might just put it as my number \#6 book to reread and learn.

### **4\. Million Dollars Weekend \- Noah Kagan**

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!pc9P!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd562e834-efb3-4639-8ffe-3e1b9fe7441c_300x457.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!pc9P!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd562e834-efb3-4639-8ffe-3e1b9fe7441c_300x457.png)

Million Dollar Weekend \- Noah Kagan

A step\-by\-step guide to building and scaling a business quickly.

The book is packed with actionables to reach your first $1,000,000 quickly. (Quickly, not easily)

### **5\. Ready, Fire, Aim \- Michael Masterson**

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!SM0E!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F107224f3-220f-40a6-8e0a-584e2119b43f_356x472.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!SM0E!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F107224f3-220f-40a6-8e0a-584e2119b43f_356x472.png)

Ready, Fire, Aim \- Michael Masterson

Masterson breaks down the key growth stages of a business and how to handle each one of them.

There are actionable steps to overcome the challenges of each phase, from zero revenue to scaling in the millions.

Here’s the gist of the formula:

**Ready** \- Prepare the product

**Fire** \- Deploy the product

**Aim** \- Adjust the product

### 3\. The Power of Habit \- Charles Duhigg

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!LipH!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F33e9fdd9-78b5-4d41-b0a1-6b3172ea2c98_348x543.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!LipH!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F33e9fdd9-78b5-4d41-b0a1-6b3172ea2c98_348x543.png)

## Honorable mention

### Storyworthy \- Matthew Dicks

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!WNas!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1b65e923-e417-41aa-b82c-28856d155e9b_336x521.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!WNas!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1b65e923-e417-41aa-b82c-28856d155e9b_336x521.png)

Storyworthy \- Matthew Dicks

I read several storytelling books in the last year. Hands down, this is the best one by far and away.

Matthew shares amazing stories from his life and explains exactly what makes you want to not miss a word he says.

The book is filled with **practical** tips to how to improve your story telling.

The easiest one to implement: **Tell your stories in present tense**.

*“I stood on the stage, looked around and saw everybody rose up from their sits and started clapping. I felt amazing.”*

vs.

*“I am standing on the stage, looking around and seeing everybody rise up from their sits and start clapping. I feel amazing”*

In which one did you feel more immersed?

## Final words

I’ll repeat what I said in the beginning, because it’s important.

**Read to learn. Don’t read to read.**

Reading for the sake of reading will not get you anywhere, unless you are looking to build the habit of reading.

If you find a great book with the knowledge you need, **learn it**, don’t just read it.
