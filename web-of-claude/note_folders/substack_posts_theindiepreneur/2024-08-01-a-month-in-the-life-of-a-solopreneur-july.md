# Products

### Chess App

At the beginning of the month, I decided to give the Chess App one last try.

The purpose of the app is to save chess club managers time by replacing manual work with automatic tasks.

After developing for a few days and giving into the fear of cold calling those managers and asking them directly, I took the step and called many clubs.

90% of them are managed by old folks who don’t care about technology and don’t want anything with it.

The rest didn’t care too much about it either.

Since it’s a large project, I decided to give up and move on.

### Sharpic

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!BHLp!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F01c27667-7abb-4f04-a5e0-76fe4b27cd49_1035x959.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!BHLp!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F01c27667-7abb-4f04-a5e0-76fe4b27cd49_1035x959.png)

This was a pretty promising idea.

After going through thousands of properties on Booking.com and Airbnb, I noticed that I clicked on those with the best images first. The rating came in second place.

So, the tool was meant to improve images for property owners to make them pop and more engaging, through Photoshop API.

A few months after I came up with the idea I decided to give it a go and build a pipeline.

But it didn’t work.

A few days after I looked into the idea, Adobe decided to practically open their API only to enterprises, which killed the idea on the spot.

I have a few ideas for how to revive it, but I’ll consider it in the future. For now, it’s in the drawer.
