### 1\. Focus on one thing at a time

This is something I have been preaching for some time now.

Ever since I started my journey, I honestly believed I can work on multiple things at the same time.

And it is possible. **But it’s not productive**.

See, when you focus on one thing and see it to its end, you’ll finish more things.

#### Practically speaking

1. Pick one product that you’re going to build. End to end.
2. Focus only on that product, no matter what.
3. Publish it and decide whether you continue improving it or move on.

Do not detour to other products, unless absolutely necessary (Bug in production that requires attention asap).

This is the first thing for a reason. **It is the most impactful one.**

### 2\. Don’t waste time. Build and ship.

**Don’t sweat the small stuff.**

And I’ll let you know why it’s more than just a catch phrase.

You probably think that your product must be perfect in order for you to ship it. Otherwise, people will notice it and will not use it.

### 3\. Social media is not just about posting

So many times people have told me to just post on several platforms at the same time, the same content, to get maximum exposure.

But it’s not just about posting.

It’s about creating connections. About engaging with other people, responding to their posts and responding to their comments.

These things take a time and a lot mental effort.

And our ability to concentrate is pretty bad as it is, so adding more possibilities for dopamine hits that will distract us is no good.

Instead, focus 99% of your energy on 1\-2 platforms.

For me, it’s Substack and X.

### 4\. You’ll have down times, it’s normal

Look, I have been through a burnout cycle more times than I can count.

It even happened a week after I took a long, 30 days break.

It comes at random times, usually after you had a good run with something and that run has come to an end.

Don’t worry. It’s completely normal.

Accept it. Let it be. Don’t force yourself to work. Don’t stress over the fact that you’re doing nothing.

Trust me, it’ll take a few days, sometimes a few weeks. But you’ll find the light again.

The more you force it, the longer it’s going to take.

### 5\. Say no more often

Opportunities will come your way. Even from people who are close to you.

You’ll have to learn to say no to things you don’t want to do.

First, it will put you, as a person, in a place in which people respect you and your yes.

Second, it will free up time for the things that truly matter to you.

Trust me, as a former people pleaser, I know how hard it is to say not and let someone down.

But, hey, if they choose to feel bad and have bad thoughts about you for taking care of yourself first..

Well, that’s on them. They need to live with it.

### 6\. LinkedIn is not the right platform to start.

After spending over 450 days on LinkedIn, I finally realized that LinkedIn is just not the right platform for entrepreneurs.

It’s for businesses and full\-time employees. So, which is the right one?

**X** and **Reddit.**

I found that so many people actually care about fellow entrepreneurs there and are interested in entrepreneurship, compared to LinkedIn.

### 7\. Don’t be productive for too long (Being obsessive on something without doing other things will get you burnt out)

Give yourself some time off. Do other things during the day.

Workout, read, write.

When you spend too much time being productive, your energy runs up so fast, that when it comes down, it will come crashing.

You’re not in it for the short, dopamine boosts. You want to sustain average\+ energy for a very long time.

#### Practically speaking

1. Don’t sit at home all day. Go out.
2. Schedule at least 4\-5 workouts a week, if you’re into it. If not, find something else that will take at least 1\-2 hours every day that keeps you in balance.
3. Have hobbies.

I know you might read it and think that it’s not possible and that you have to work.

Trust me. I’ve been through way too many cycles of depression at this stage that, so believe me when I say: **It’s not worth it.**

It’s better if you keep your energy on average\+ rather than have it scattered all around.

**That means \- no overworking.**

### 8\. Read. Don’t give it up.

Reading is one of the most **overestimated and underutilized** habits when it comes to building yourself as a solopreneur.

Everyone talks about reading, but few actually do it consistently.

And many of those who do it consistently, do it just for the sake of reading and not to get valuable knowledge.

I mean, if you read for fun, that’s cool and you should keep doing that. I, personally, after reading over 220 books, didn’t get to the point in which I enjoy reading books.

And if you read, like me, to get valuable knowledge, here’s the best way to do it:

1. Figure out what you want or need to learn
2. Pick up the best books on the subject
3. Skim all of them, **learn** the best of them.

By learning I mean read, reread and reread again if needed.

The list of books I read didn’t grow in the last 1\.5 months because I am rereading these 5 books:

1. 100M offers, by Alex Hormozi
2. 100M leads, by Alex Hormozi
3. Dotcom secrets, by Russell Brunson
4. Experts Secrets, by Russell Brunson
5. Traffic Secrets, by Russell Brunson

Why? Because I need to learn how to market and get clients.

Why these books? I trust Hormozi and Hormozi learned a lot from Russell Brunson.

### 9\. Niche. Niche. Niche. (Don’t talk to many people. Find your attractive character and talk to them)

If you can’t understand yet what niching down means, that’s fine. I didn’t know either.

I thought it’s just a fancy word smart people use. I’ll try to explain it as if I am explaining it to my younger self.

* Quitting your job is a niche.
* Entrepreneurship is a niche.
* Building products fast is a niche.
* Building embedded, physical products is a niche.
* Writing code using only the weirdest tech is a niche.

Literally everything is a niche.

You need to find the thing that interests you the most, and talk and write about it.

There are probably very few things in the world that don’t have a crowd.

And the more niched down and specific the niche is, the better.

**Why?** Because when you are an expert at solving a very specific problem to someone, for example: “Dev agency that only builds Cyber products on Linux, using C and ASM”, you have **A LOT** of leverage.

You can charge almost as much as you want for this service.

The amount of customers is low, but each one can be extremely valuable.

### 10\. Enjoy the journey \- Not what you think

I’ve grown to disdain the phrase “Enjoy the journey”, because I believe it is misinterpreted.

Think about good things that happened to you in life.

* You found the love of your life
* You built a product people are willing to pay for
* You’ve hit your goal weight

None of these things were easy.

Heck, I have been working out for over 1\.5 years straight and I still fight the demons every single time I have to get up and go the the gym.

It’s even worse with meditation, which I’ve been doing almost consistently for the past 4 years.

**But enjoy the journey is not about the now.**

It’s about looking back and feeling good and happy about the results and what all the hard work led to.

It might’ve been incredibly “not enjoyable” at that moment, but it was worth it.

Let me summarize it:

**Enjoy the journey is not about the here and now. It’s about doing things that you know, in the future, you’ll be happy you did.**
