**1:** Another charity fundraiser, this one for **[Lightcone Infrastructure](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/eKGdCNdKjvTBG9i6y/toss-a-bitcoin-to-your-lightcone-lw-lighthaven-s-2026)**. Lightcone is the group that does the hard work for many of the rationalist community resources you enjoy. You probably know them from the [Less Wrong](https://www.lesswrong.com/) website and the [Lighthaven](https://www.lighthaven.space/) campus. But did you know they also designed the websites for [AI 2027](https://ai-2027.com/), for [Eliezer and Nate’s book](https://ifanyonebuildsit.com/), for [AI Lab Watch](https://ailabwatch.org/), and (for some reason) for [Deciding To Win](https://decidingtowin.org/), a renegade faction of Democrats who believe that, instead of supporting unpopular policies and losing, the party should support popular policies and win? And on the side, they play a big role in hosting ACX meetups, including letting us use their campus (if you’ve ever been to our Berkeley meetup location, that was them). They’re a rare intersection between “support effective altruist charities” and “support pillars of your your local community”. Donate [here](https://www.every.org/lightcone-infrastructure?suggestedAmounts=50%2C100%2C1000%2C2000%2C10000&theme_color=7faf83&designation=Lightcone+Infrastructure&utm_campaign=donate-link#/donate/card), or [contact Oli](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/eKGdCNdKjvTBG9i6y/toss-a-bitcoin-to-your-lightcone-lw-lighthaven-s-2026) if you have some kind of more complicated donation\-related need.

**2:** A bio policy group asks me to signal\-boost their request for a volunteer to help them with vaccine policy research:

> [News reports indicate](https://www.cnn.com/2025/12/18/health/hhs-childhood-vaccine-changes) that Secretary Kennedy plans to replace the U.S. childhood vaccine schedule with Denmark’s, the developed country that recommends the fewest vaccines. This would reduce American children’s access to rotavirus, meningitis, hepatitis A, hepatitis B, influenza, RSV, and chickenpox vaccines. We are looking for researchers to help [Stanley Plotkin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stanley_Plotkin) (professor emeritus and co\-inventor of the rubella vaccine) this week with a [publication](https://docs.google.com/document/d/19qxp3YWbp4jQk6acpQAH5aZcipPfRs3FZ_Eq8HSnCUE/edit?tab=t.0) that aims to estimate the medical consequences if American doctors stop prescribing these vaccines. This would involve about 4\-10 hours of work. If you are interested, **[please fill out this form](https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLScsvHkWM7uAAD5W24fQBlMoRc9yef-AN6OYAJvea69xYDNmdg/viewform?usp=header)**.

**3:** AI safety org MIRI is running a [“technical governance team research fellowship”](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/Dom6E2CCaH6qxqwAY/announcing-miri-technical-governance-team-research) in early 2026\. Technical governance is at the intersection of engineering and regulation, and includes things like designing chips with cryptographic off switches, or analyzing US and international law to see what levers different groups have to monitor AI training. The fellowship lasts 8 weeks (exact dates tbd but flexible), pays a $1200/week stipend, and will start with a one\-week intro in Berkeley (flights/accommodations provided) followed by seven weeks potentially remote. No visa sponsorship. See here for more info or to apply.
